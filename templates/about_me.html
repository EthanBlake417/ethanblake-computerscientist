About Me

Hello! I am Ethan Blake. I grew up in Phoenix Arizona and come from a large family of 10. I am the fifth child out of eight, so I ended up being the hobbyist of the family, spending most of my childhood playing sports and music. In fact, due to a nice scholarship offer I decided to go to Arizona State University for a bachelor's degree in vocal performance. In 2018, I graduated (and got married!), but struggled to find consistent income in music, so I ended up working other odd jobs, including an assembly line job for Axon (Taser) and a sales job for State Farm.

Soon after I graduated with my degree in Vocal performance and got married, I was involved in a traumatic car accident. In fact, the passenger of the driver that hit me ended up dying! I came out okay, except I sustained some significant neck and brain damage which manifest themselves in nonsensical pains I feel in my face and neck. Because of the nonsensical pains I haven't been able to sing without significant pain since the accident. The pain, combined with working a few jobs that I disliked motivated me to find a new career.

When the coronavirus pandemic hit in 2020 I lost my job working for Axon, so I decided I might go to a coding bootcamp, but as things turned out I was able to pursue a Post-Baccalaureate at Oregon State University in Computer Science, while I worked for Grid Evolution Technologies in a programming role.

Looking back, I never would have wanted that car accident to happen; I still deal with some nerve pain, and I really miss singing, but in a lot of ways, I'm so thankful for the hard circumstances that lead me to get a Bachelor's in computer science. I've always enjoyed skill building and learning, whether the skill is as simple as jumping rope, as complicated as singing, or as cool as solving puzzles. I've found programming to be about as fun as singing and perhaps even more satisfying.

Currently, I'm most interested in either Machine Learning or Computer Engineering, but in all honesty as long as I am learning new things I enjoy just about everything in the CS world.
